Diego Colnago

Tired of being a legal analyst, I started to learn code to become a developer.

I graduated in law school around 10 years ago and I have been working as a public servant for more than twelve years. Life is good in general, but I don't have the same feeling about my job that I used to have. I constantly see myself counting the minutes to end the workday, counting the months to the next vacation and counting the years to retirement. I feel bad about it.

I don't feel well doing what I do for living anymore. It is just a boring task that I do to get the money I need for living.

Learning to code brought me back the excitement and the joy to work on something. It may be only a hobby for now, but it is opening my eyes to a new world of possibilities and it makes me feel great.
